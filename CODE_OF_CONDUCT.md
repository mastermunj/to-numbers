# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers commit to making participation in to-numbers and its community a respectful, safe, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We commit to acting and interacting in ways that support an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that help build a positive environment include:

- Using respectful and inclusive language
- Accepting and giving constructive feedback in good faith
- Focusing on what is best for the project and the wider community
- Showing empathy and patience toward other contributors

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwanted sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing someone else's private information without explicit permission
- Any other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct. When practical, they will explain moderation decisions.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other public or private channels used to represent or coordinate work on to-numbers.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the maintainer directly using the contact email listed on their GitHub profile. Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as confidentially as possible.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may apply the following guidelines when determining an appropriate response:

### 1. Correction

Impact: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior that is unprofessional or unwelcome.

Consequence: A private correction explaining the issue and what behavior is expected going forward.

### 2. Warning

Impact: A single serious incident or a pattern of problematic behavior.

Consequence: A formal warning. Continued behavior may result in a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

Impact: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

Consequence: A temporary ban from community interaction for a defined period.

### 4. Permanent Ban

Impact: A pattern of violations, targeted harassment, or aggressive behavior toward individuals or groups.

Consequence: Permanent removal from public interaction within the project community.
